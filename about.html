About Ommani Alex

Designing for real life, not perfect conditions.

I am a user experience design student and the founder of Ommani Collective, a values-driven studio focused on creating accessible, adaptable systems and digital experiences.

My work is shaped by lived experience, practical problem-solving, and a deep belief that technology should make life easier to navigate, not harder.

My focus

I create systems that reduce friction, support different ways of thinking, and help people feel less overwhelmed when interacting with digital tools.

Core Values

Accessibility and compassion

I believe access is not optional. Systems should work for people with different cognitive styles, energy levels, and lived experiences. Compassion matters because good design starts with understanding what people are actually dealing with before trying to solve the problem.

Strengths

Systems thinking and problem-solving

I naturally notice where processes break down, where people get stuck, and what needs to be clarified. That helps me design workflows and digital spaces that feel easier to use and easier to maintain.

My approach

Clear

I prioritize structure, readable content, and simple next steps so people do not have to fight the design to understand what to do.

Flexible

I design with real-world inconsistency in mind, including low energy days, distraction, stress, and changing needs.

Human

I ask how a system feels to use, not just whether it technically works. That question shapes the way I build.

What shaped this work

Working in veterinary clinics during major software transitions showed me how much system design affects people’s ability to function. When workflows are unclear, even capable teams can feel overwhelmed. When systems are thoughtful, visual, and well-structured, the work becomes easier to manage. That experience still informs how I think about accessibility, change, and user support.
